# Welcome to Steel Hawks Electrical

Welcome to the electrical documentation for **FRC Team 2601 - Steel Hawks**! This section covers everything you need to know about wiring, power distribution, and the electronics that bring our competition robots to life.

## Getting Started

No prior electrical experience is required to join — but you will need to be careful, methodical, and willing to learn. A robot that isn't wired correctly won't move, no matter how good the code or the mechanism is.

The electrical subteam owns the path from the battery to every motor, sensor, and controller on the robot.

## Electrical is About Trust

Every other subteam depends on the electrical board working. Mechanical builds the mechanism, programming writes the code — but if a connector is loose or a wire is undersized, none of it matters. **The work has to be clean, labeled, and reliable.**

That means:

- Crimps that don't pull out
- Connectors that are seated and strain-relieved
- A board that's documented so anyone can trace a fault
- Respecting the electrical limits — fuses, breakers, and wire gauges exist for a reason

## What You'll Learn

This curriculum will grow over time. Planned topics include:

- Robot electrical overview — the battery, PDH/PDP, and main breaker
- Motor controllers and CAN wiring
- Sensors and signal wiring
- Pneumatics control
- Wiring standards and best practices
- Troubleshooting a dead or misbehaving robot

## Ready to Start?

Work through the topics in the sidebar in order. When you finish a topic, talk to a lead on the electrical subteam — they'll get you hands-on with the real board.

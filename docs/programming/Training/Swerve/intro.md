# Swerve & Odometry Introduction

Welcome to the next section! In this section we're going to cover one of the most important subsystems: <span style={{color: '#8f0f0f', fontWeight: 'bold'}}>Swerve</span>

Swerve is what your robot uses to move, and is part of the mechanical **drivetrain**. These terms will be defined later, so don't worry about not knowing everything right now.

Alongside swerve, we'll also introduce **odometry** &rarr; the system that lets the robot keep track of where it is on the field as it drives around. Swerve and odometry go hand in hand: one lets the robot move in any direction, and the other lets the robot know exactly where it ended up after moving.

We won't be diving into every detail of these two topics just yet &rarr; that's what the rest of this section is for. For now, here's a preview of what you'll learn:
